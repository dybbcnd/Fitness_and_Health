Frequently Asked Questions

How much exercise should I do each week to stay healthy?

Most health organizations recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week, along with muscle-strengthening activities twice a week. This can be broken down into smaller sessions throughout the week.

What's the best diet for weight loss and overall health?

There's no single "best" diet, but a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, and healthy fats is generally recommended. Focus on reducing processed foods, sugary drinks, and unhealthy fats. Consulting a registered dietitian can help personalize a plan for your needs.

How can I stay motivated to stick to my fitness goals?

Find activities you enjoy, set realistic and achievable goals, track your progress, reward yourself for milestones (non-food rewards are best!), find a workout buddy for support, and don't be afraid to adjust your plan if needed. Consistency is key.

Is it okay to exercise when I'm sick?

Generally, it's best to rest when you're sick, especially if you have a fever, cough, or body aches. Light activity, like a short walk, might be okay if you only have mild symptoms above the neck (like a runny nose), but listen to your body. If you feel worse after exercising, stop immediately. Pushing yourself when sick can prolong your illness and potentially lead to complications.

How can I prevent injuries while working out?

Proper warm-up and cool-down routines are essential. Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Use correct form during exercises – consider working with a trainer to learn proper technique. Listen to your body and don't push yourself too hard, especially when starting a new exercise program. Wear appropriate footwear and clothing, and ensure your workout environment is safe. Rest and recovery are also crucial for injury prevention.
